What DigiFab AI brings to your line

  • Real-time risk scoring for pores, lack of fusion, balling, and geometry drift.
  • Sensor fusion across optical, thermal, and acoustic channels.
  • APIs and control hooks that integrate with existing machines instead of replacing them.

Designed for aerospace, energy, and industrial users who need traceable, certifiable builds – not just pretty heat maps.

Why this matters for production AM

Most metal AM lines still treat quality control as a post-build activity. By the time CT or ultrasound tells you something is wrong, the part and the time are already gone. DigiFab AI moves quality into the loop, next to your toolpath.

Catch defects while you can still act

Monitor keyhole stability, melt pool health, and process anomalies at frame-level resolution so you can pause, adjust, or scrap early instead of discovering problems days later.

Generalize beyond one alloy or one machine

Our models learn representations that transfer across alloys, parameter sets, and even machine types, reducing the cost of deploying AI at scale.

Build a data asset, not a dashboard

Structured logs, explainable alerts, and clear audit trails make it easier to plug into MES, PLM, and certification workflows instead of creating yet another silo.

How the DigiFab AI copilot fits into your workflow

We work with the sensors and control interfaces you already have. The copilot ingests your signals, learns your process, and surfaces actionable insights without forcing you to re-architect the line.

1. Observe

Connect cameras, photodiodes, pyrometers, acoustic emission, and log streams. We align and synchronize signals from microseconds to layers.

LPBF DED / WAAM

2. Understand

Vision transformers and temporal models transform raw sensor data into predictions of pore formation, spatter regimes, and risk scores for each region of the build.

Keyhole dynamics Process windows

3. Act

The copilot can trigger alerts, flag regions for inspection, or send parameter nudges through your existing control systems, enabling gradual automation instead of a big bang.
